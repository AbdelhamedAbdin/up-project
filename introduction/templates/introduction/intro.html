Welcome to the second phase of The Corpus of Mid-20th Century Hong Kong Cantonese.

In 2012, with the support of an internal research grant from the Education University of Hong Kong (the then Hong Kong Institute of Education), we developed the first phase of The Corpus of Mid-20th Century Hong Kong Cantonese. The corpus was designed to provide language data for studying the earlier stage of Hong Kong Cantonese.

The corpus data was collected by transcribing the dialogues of Cantonese movies produced in Hong Kong between the 1940s and the 1970s. 21 movies were transcribed and 14 of them were made available online.

In 2013, we obtained a research grant under the Early Career Scheme (ECS) from the Research Grants Council to further develop the second phase of our corpus. Altogether 60 movies were transcribed, with about 770,000 character tokens. The movies our project team selected are balanced in terms of genre and speakers (including gender) so that the corpus data can represent the Cantonese language spoken in the mid-20th century.

In this website, you can find more information about the corpus, such as the list of movies and speakers; some basic information about the corpus, a user guide of the search engine.

We are still working on some technical issues and will periodically make updates to the site. Please stay tuned.

We hope you enjoy using our corpus and find the data useful. If you have any queries, please contact us at hkcc@eduhk.hk.

Andy Chin

Principal Investigator
The Corpus of Mid-20th Century Hong Kong Cantonese

The following is a list of new features added to the data of the second phase of the corpus:

  1. Words are segmented and assigned a parts-of-speech (POS) tag. In other words, the corpus now can distinguish between 「一」 as a numeral and as an adverb;
  2. Jyutping and POS tags will be provided together with the transcription text;
  3. The transcription was conducted with the ELAN freeware developed by Max Planck Institute for Psycholinguistics. With ELAN, we were able to find the exact time point where the utterance was made in the movies. Therefore, besides textual transcriptions, users can also view the video segments of their search results. This can provide more modal information such as gesture, speed, prosody that is associated with the utterance.

The second phase of the corpus has around 5,200 character types, totaling around 770,000 tokens.

It is interesting to note that the 50 most frequent characters already cover 50% of the corpus data.

To have a coverage of 90% and 95%, the numbers of highest frequency characters are 500 and 840 respectively.
